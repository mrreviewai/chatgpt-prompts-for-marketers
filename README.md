## 🔥 Sales Pages


High-converting sales pages follow a structure. These prompts bake the structure in.


---


### The Full Sales Page (AIDA Framework)


```
Write a long-form sales page for [PRODUCT NAME] using the AIDA framework.

Product: [PRODUCT NAME]
What it does: [1-2 sentences describing what it does]
Target customer: [WHO they are, their job/situation]
Main problem it solves: [THE PAIN — be specific]
Price: [PRICE]
Guarantee: [GUARANTEE if any]
Key benefits (list 3-5): [BENEFIT 1], [BENEFIT 2], [BENEFIT 3]
Social proof available: [testimonials / number of customers / results]

Structure the page as:
- Headline (bold, problem-focused)
- Subheadline (what changes after buying)
- The problem section (agitate the pain)
- The solution (introduce the product)
- Benefits list (what they get)
- Social proof / testimonials
- What's included (features)
- Guarantee section
- Price and CTA
- FAQ (5 most common objections)

Tone: [conversational / professional / urgent]
```


---


### The Problem-Agitate-Solution (PAS) Sales Page


```
Write a sales page using the PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution) copywriting framework.

Product: [PRODUCT NAME]
Target customer: [WHO they are]
Their #1 problem: [THE SPECIFIC PAIN POINT]
What makes this problem worse: [WHY it's painful / what they've already tried]
My solution: [HOW the product fixes it]
Proof: [RESULTS, testimonials, or data]
CTA: [WHAT you want them to do — buy, book a call, sign up]

Make the Problem section feel like you're reading their mind.
Make the Agitation section feel uncomfortable — show the cost of inaction.
Make the Solution feel like relief.
```


---


### Short Sales Page (for low-ticket or digital products)


```
Write a short, punchy sales page (under 500 words) for [PRODUCT NAME].

Product: [NAME]
Price: [PRICE]
What it is: [1 sentence]
Who it's for: [TARGET AUDIENCE]
The result they get: [SPECIFIC OUTCOME]
Main objection to address: [BIGGEST DOUBT a buyer has]

Include: headline, 3 bullet benefits, social proof placeholder, price reveal, single CTA button.
No fluff. Every sentence must earn its place.
```


---


### FAQ Section for Any Sales Page


```
Write an FAQ section for a sales page selling [PRODUCT NAME] at [PRICE].

Target customer: [WHO they are]
Common objections: [list 3-5 doubts a potential buyer might have]
Guarantee: [if any]

Write 8-10 FAQ questions and answers. Make the answers short, honest, and confident.
Address: price objection, "is this for me?" objection, results timeline, refund policy, and any product-specific doubts.
```

## 📧 Email Marketing


Emails that get opened. Sequences that convert.


---


### Welcome Email Sequence (5 emails)


```
Write a 5-email welcome sequence for new subscribers who signed up for [LEAD MAGNET / FREEBIE].

Brand/product: [NAME]
What I sell: [PRODUCT or SERVICE]
Subscriber goal: [WHAT THEY WANT — not what you sell]
Email 1 (Day 0): Deliver the freebie + warm welcome
Email 2 (Day 1): Share one quick win related to their goal
Email 3 (Day 3): Tell my story — why I built this, who I am
Email 4 (Day 5): Address the biggest objection to buying [PRODUCT]
Email 5 (Day 7): Make the offer with a soft CTA

Tone: [conversational / expert / friendly]
Keep each email under 300 words. Subject lines should create curiosity, not reveal everything.
```


---


### Cold Email (B2B Outreach)


```
Write a cold email to [JOB TITLE] at [TYPE OF COMPANY].

What I offer: [PRODUCT/SERVICE in 1 sentence]
Their likely pain point: [SPECIFIC PROBLEM they have]
My proof: [RESULT I've gotten for similar companies]
CTA: [SPECIFIC — book 15-min call, reply to this email, etc.]

Rules:
- Subject line: under 8 words, no hype
- Email body: under 150 words
- No "I hope this finds you well"
- Lead with THEIR problem, not my product
- One CTA only
```


---


### Re-engagement Email (for cold subscribers)


```
Write a re-engagement email for subscribers who haven't opened in 90+ days.

Brand: [NAME]
What I normally send: [TYPE OF CONTENT]
Offer to re-engage them: [FREEBIE, discount, exclusive content, or just ask]

Two versions:
Version A: Funny / self-aware ("We haven't talked in a while...")
Version B: Direct / value-first ("Here's something new I think you'll actually want")

End both with an unsubscribe option — frame it positively ("Only stay if this is useful to you").
```


---


### Subject Line Generator


```
Write 15 email subject lines for an email about [TOPIC] targeting [AUDIENCE].

Use a different hook for each:
- Curiosity gap (3 subject lines)
- Specific number or result (3 subject lines)
- Contrarian / unexpected angle (2 subject lines)
- Personal / story-based (2 subject lines)
- Urgency (2 subject lines)
- Question (2 subject lines)
- Emoji-led (1 subject line)

Keep all under 50 characters. Flag the top 3 you'd test first.
```

## 📣 Social Media


Posts that get saved, shared, and followed.


---


### LinkedIn Post (Thought Leadership)


```
Write a LinkedIn post sharing a lesson I learned from [EXPERIENCE / RESULT / MISTAKE].

My lesson: [THE INSIGHT in 1 sentence]
Story behind it: [WHAT HAPPENED — brief context]
Who this helps: [TARGET AUDIENCE on LinkedIn]
My business/angle: [WHAT I DO — so the post feels authentic]

Format:
- Hook line (no "I'm excited to share" — make it a pattern interrupt)
- 3-5 short paragraphs, max 2 sentences each
- Insight or takeaway
- Question to drive comments
- No hashtag spam (max 3 relevant tags)

Tone: honest, direct, no corporate speak
```


---


### Twitter/X Thread


```
Write a Twitter/X thread about [TOPIC].

Main insight: [THE BIG IDEA in 1 sentence]
Target audience: [WHO will find this useful]
My angle/experience: [WHY I'm credible on this topic]

Format:
- Tweet 1: Hook (bold claim, surprising fact, or contrarian take — max 280 chars)
- Tweets 2-8: One idea per tweet, build on each other
- Tweet 9: Summary / "If you take one thing from this..."
- Tweet 10: CTA (follow, retweet, check out [link])

Each tweet must work as a standalone. No "in this thread I will..." No filler.
```


---


### Instagram / Facebook Caption


```
Write a social media caption for a post about [TOPIC / IMAGE DESCRIPTION].

Platform: [Instagram / Facebook / Threads]
Goal: [engagement / reach / clicks / followers]
My niche: [WHAT I'M KNOWN FOR]
Audience: [WHO follows me]

Format:
- Hook line (first 2 lines — must stop the scroll)
- Body: valuable insight, story, or tip (3-5 short paragraphs)
- CTA: [comment / save / DM / link in bio]
- Hashtags: 5-10 relevant ones (for Instagram)

Tone: [educational / entertaining / inspirational / conversational]
```

## 💰 Paid Ads


Ad copy that converts without sounding like an ad.


---


### Facebook / Meta Ad (3 variations)


```
Write 3 Facebook ad variations for [PRODUCT/SERVICE].

Product: [NAME]
Price: [PRICE or "free"]
Target audience: [WHO sees this ad — interests, job, situation]
Main pain point: [WHAT KEEPS THEM UP AT NIGHT]
Main benefit: [THE RESULT they get]
CTA: [Shop Now / Learn More / Sign Up / Book a Call]

Variation 1: Problem-focused (lead with pain)
Variation 2: Result-focused (lead with the transformation)
Variation 3: Social proof-focused (lead with a customer result or number)

For each: write Primary Text (under 150 words) + Headline (under 40 chars) + Description (under 30 chars).
```


---


### Google Search Ad


```
Write Google Search ad copy for the keyword "[TARGET KEYWORD]".

Product/service: [NAME]
USP (why choose us): [ONE differentiator]
Offer: [discount / free trial / guarantee]
CTA: [ACTION]

Write:
- 3 headlines (max 30 characters each)
- 2 descriptions (max 90 characters each)
- 5 sitelink extensions with descriptions

Match the search intent of someone typing "[TARGET KEYWORD]" — they want [WHAT THEY WANT], not just information.
```

## 🎯 Landing Pages


Landing pages that do one job: convert.


---


### Lead Magnet Landing Page


```
Write a landing page for a free [LEAD MAGNET TYPE] called "[LEAD MAGNET NAME]".

Who it's for: [TARGET AUDIENCE]
What they get: [SPECIFIC DELIVERABLE — be exact]
The result they get from using it: [OUTCOME, not just "value"]
What they give: [email only / name + email]

Include:
- Headline (what they get + who it's for)
- 3-5 bullet points (specific things inside the freebie)
- One-line social proof or credibility statement
- Email capture form copy
- Privacy line ("No spam. Unsubscribe anytime.")

Under 300 words total. Every word earns its place.
```


---


### Waitlist / Coming Soon Page


```
Write copy for a waitlist landing page for [PRODUCT NAME — not yet launched].

What it does: [WHAT IT DOES in 1 sentence]
Who it's for: [AUDIENCE]
Launch date (or "coming soon"): [DATE / QUARTER]
Benefit of joining early: [EARLY ACCESS / DISCOUNT / BONUS]

Include:
- Headline (creates curiosity without revealing everything)
- 3 teaser bullet points (hint at features without full detail)
- Email capture copy ("Join the waitlist — [X] people already signed up")
- Urgency element if any
```

## ✍️ Hooks & Headlines


The first line is everything. If it doesn't hook, nothing else matters.


---


### 20 Hooks for Any Topic


```
Write 20 opening hooks for content about [TOPIC] targeting [AUDIENCE].

Use these hook types (2-3 of each):
- Bold contrarian claim ("Most [X] advice is wrong. Here's why.")
- Specific number ("I went from [X] to [Y] in [timeframe]. Here's how.")
- "What nobody tells you about [TOPIC]"
- Question that creates self-doubt ("Are you making this [TOPIC] mistake?")
- Surprising fact or stat
- Story opener ("In [year], I [ACTION]. It changed everything.")
- Before/after ("Before: [BAD STATE]. After: [GOOD STATE].")
- Counterintuitive result ("I [STOPPED DOING THING] and my results doubled.")

All hooks must work as the first line of a post, email subject, or headline.
```


---


### Headline Rewriter


```
Rewrite this headline 10 ways: "[YOUR CURRENT HEADLINE]"

Target reader: [WHO reads this]
What they want: [DESIRED OUTCOME]
Their fear: [WHAT THEY DON'T WANT]

For each rewrite, use a different angle:
1. Curiosity
2. Specific result + timeframe
3. Social proof
4. "How to" format
5. Negative angle (what to avoid)
6. Question
7. Direct / blunt
8. Contrarian
9. Before/after
10. Number-led

Label each one. Flag which 3 you'd test first.
```

## 🔍 SEO & Content


Content that ranks and gets read.


---


### Full Blog Post (SEO-Optimized)


```
Write a 1,500-word SEO blog post targeting the keyword "[TARGET KEYWORD]".

Topic: [TOPIC]
Target reader: [WHO they are, what they know]
Search intent: [WHAT they want from this article — info / comparison / how-to]
My angle / unique perspective: [WHAT MAKES THIS DIFFERENT from the top 10 results]

Structure:
- H1: include keyword naturally
- Intro: hook + what they'll learn (no "In this article I will...")
- H2 sections: [LIST 4-5 subtopics]
- Include 1 "pro tip" box
- CTA at the end: [WHAT YOU WANT THEM TO DO NEXT]

Write in [TONE — conversational / expert / neutral].
Avoid keyword stuffing. Write for humans first, SEO second.
```

### Content Repurposing Machine


```
I have a [BLOG POST / VIDEO / PODCAST EPISODE] about [TOPIC].

Repurpose it into:
1. LinkedIn post (thought leadership angle, 200-300 words)
2. Twitter/X thread (10 tweets)

4. Email newsletter intro (150 words — tease the full content with a link)

[PASTE YOUR CONTENT HERE]

Keep the core insight consistent across all formats. Adapt the tone for each platform.
```

## 🤝 Outreach & Cold DM


Messages that get replies, not ignored.


---


### Cold DM (Twitter/X or LinkedIn)


```
Write a cold DM to [PERSON'S ROLE] about [WHAT I WANT — collaboration / feedback / sale].

Context: [WHY I'M REACHING OUT — what I noticed about them specifically]
What I offer: [VALUE I can give THEM first]
The ask: [SMALL, SPECIFIC request — not "let's hop on a call"]

Rules:
- Under 100 words
- Start with something specific about THEM (not generic flattery)
- Give value before asking
- One ask only, make it easy to say yes
- No "I hope this message finds you well"
```


---


### Partnership / Collab Pitch


```
Write a partnership pitch email to [PERSON / BRAND] about [TYPE OF COLLAB].

My platform: [WHAT I DO, audience size, niche]
Their platform: [WHAT THEY DO]
Why this works for THEM: [WHAT THEY GET — be specific about their benefit]
What I'm proposing: [EXACT TYPE OF COLLAB — guest post, cross-promo, co-create, etc.]
My ask: [FIRST SMALL STEP — "reply if interested" / "15-min call"]

Keep it under 200 words. Make the benefit to them obvious in the first 3 lines.
```

## The Golden Rule of AI Prompts


> Garbage in, garbage out.
>
> The AI is only as good as your brief. Treat it like briefing a talented junior copywriter who knows nothing about your business. The more context you give — who the customer is, what they feel, what they fear, what they want — the better the output.
>
> A good prompt has: Role (who the AI is) + Context (the situation) + Task (what to write) + Format (how to structure it) + Constraints (tone, length, rules).
